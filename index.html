5 Reasons For Using Zoho Writer

Effective communication is crucial for a business to succeed. Be it external or internal, it showcases the business’ persona to the world. But handling such a crucial task of 'perfecting the words' can prove tedious and time-consuming. Valuable time that can be spent on other tasks that can improve your business.

So it’s always best to leave the art of perfecting your business writing in the hands of a professional writing tool like Zoho Writer that has zero tolerance for errors and can help your executives (including your writers) communicate better with the world.

Here are 5 reasons for using Zoho Writer - if you are not already using it.


1. Interface That Inspires: Writing with a clear head can be tough when you are bombarded with a host of icons or cards offering unnecessary suggestions. With Zoho Writer, we promise it will be just you and your words. A simple advantage that can make a world of difference to your corporate writing.

2. Artificial Intelligence Backed Suggestions: It’s only natural to make mistakes when you write. In Zia, the artificial intelligence backed assistant, you get a friend who’s always there to bail your business out of embarrassing writing related errors. She offers just the right amount of assistance that can take your business to a whole new level.

3. Ready to Use Templates: Writing from scratch is not always a feasible option. So you are presented with a host of ready-to-use templates that cater to everyday tasks a business might undertake. All you have to do is just select one and get started.

4. Workflow Automation Every business is unique and might have some requirements which the default settings of Zoho Writer might not be able to meet. That is why the word processing tool provides you with automation templates - that not only help you automate your organization’s documentation workflows, but also bring your native content (content specific to your organization) onboard seamlessly.

5. Multi Device and Offline Support: Zoho Writer allows businesses to write across devices - be it laptop, smartphone or tab. You just need your Zoho login credentials to get started. Above all, you get offline support, something which GoogleDocs and Microsoft Word don’t yet offer.

Automation in Zoho Writer

Automation in Zoho Writer refers to the task of automating your document workflows. For example, let's say you are a recruiting agency that hires hundreds of employees for your clients. This means mailing out hundreds of candidate application forms (each with different sets of form fields), depending on your client's needs.

What if you could automate such a tedious task of manually creating those client-specific form fields? Well, with Zoho Writer, you could do just that - and much more.

Zoho Writer offers 3 types of automation:


1. Merge Templates: This is what we just talked about. The ability to prefill your documents with client-specific jargon or lingo or simply 'field names' unique to the client. They are called 'merge templates' because you are basically 'merging' or 'importing' client-specific fields into your existing Zoho Writer form templates.

This import can happen from one of the following data sources: csv files or json files or Zoho Contacts or Zoho Sheet or Zoho Forms or Zoho Creator or Zoho CRM, depending on where you choose to store your client information.


2. Fillable Templates: While working with clients, you have to get their feedback from time to time to understand whether you are meeting their requirements or not. So you need survey forms or questionnaires to accomplish this. Again, this means different sets of forms or questionnaires for different sets of clients.

Creation and distribution of such 'fillable forms or questionnaires' too can be automated using 'fillable templates' in Zoho Writer, rather than having to create them manually for each and every client.


3. Sign Templates: 'Sign Collection' is integral to documentation workflows. It could be the signature of the new hire or a top-level executive that needs to be collected to finish off a particular document's workflow. It's best to automate the contents of these documents because they usually have to be distributed to several people with the same content.

Using 'Sign Templates' of Zoho Writer, you can easily automate the creation and distribution of such sign collection documents, removing the need for having to create them manually.


Now that you know how automation in Zoho Writer can make your lives easier. Go ahead and incorporate them into your organization's documentation workflows and improve your business.
